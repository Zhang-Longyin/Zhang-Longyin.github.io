About

I am a Senior Scientist I at IAIC, A*STAR. Before IAIC was formed through the merger of I2R and IHPC on July 1, 2026, I worked at the Institute for Infocomm Research (I2R), A*STAR, where I contributed to MERaLiON-related research on multilingual, multimodal, and culturally grounded foundation models for Southeast Asia.

I received my Ph.D. from the NLP Research Group of Soochow University, China, under the supervision of Prof. Fang Kong and Prof. Guodong Zhou.

My research started from Discourse Analysis, Entailment Reasoning, and Summarization, and has recently expanded to multimodal LLMs, omni-perception models, perception-reasoning trade-offs, and long-video temporal reasoning. I am open to research collaborations on discourse-aware NLP, multimodal reasoning, culturally grounded AI, and temporal reasoning.

Email: zhangly@i2r.a-star.edu.sg

Research Focus

Discourse-aware NLP

Discourse parsing, discourse-aware generation, summarization, and entailment reasoning beyond isolated sentences.

Culturally-aware Multimodal LLMs

SEA-focused models and benchmarks that capture local visual, acoustic, linguistic, and cultural signals.

MERaLiON-Omni-MM-10B MERaLiON-Omni-GRPO-10B

Perception-Reasoning Trade-offs

Studying how System-2-style reasoning improves abstract tasks while causing temporal drift or visual over-interpretation.

arXiv:2602.23730

Cross-modal Temporal Reasoning

Event-aware long-video reasoning with physical-time grounding, audio-visual retrieval, and efficient temporal attention.
